# Team Photos — Visual Descriptions
**From:** Prospero
**For:** Cicero
**Project:** Team Photos

Working notes for each team member. These are character studies, not finished prose — use them as raw material for the Midjourney prompts. The aesthetic throughout is 1960s surveillance photography: grainy black and white film, candid framing, high contrast, a sense that the subject doesn't know they're being watched.

---

## Adama — Orchestrator

Late 50s. Silver-haired, close-cropped. The kind of face that has absorbed a great deal and given little back. Not physically imposing but unmistakably authoritative — there is something in the set of his jaw that says he has already made the decision everyone else is still discussing.

Photographed from across a rain-slicked street, through a car windshield. He is standing on the pavement outside a nondescript building, hands in the pockets of a dark overcoat, looking left — not searching, just watching. His posture is at ease, but precise. Like a man who is always exactly where he means to be.

No hat. Collar up slightly against the cold. The image is slightly soft-focus at the edges — telephoto lens at its limit.

## Prospero — Senior Researcher

Mid-50s. Lean, angular, the kind of thinness that comes from forgetting to eat. Wire-rimmed glasses, pushed up slightly as if recently consulted. A scholar's face: high forehead, thoughtful lines around the eyes. Not imposing, but the sort of man you wouldn't want to underestimate across a table.

Photographed through a library window — he is seated at a long table covered in documents, caught in profile. His head is slightly tilted, one hand resting on an open file, the other raised with a pen mid-thought. He appears to be reading something deeply disagreeable and finding it fascinating anyway.

Jacket and tie, both slightly loosened. A half-drunk cup of something at his elbow. The venetian blind behind him casts striped shadows across the frame.

## Ocean — HR Agent

Early 40s. Composed, elegant without ostentation. The face of someone who has walked into every room she's entered and immediately known more about its occupants than they knew about each other. Dark hair, pulled back cleanly. Eyes that are warm on the surface and calibrating beneath.

Photographed in what appears to be a hotel lobby — she is mid-stride, carrying a slim folder, caught in a three-quarter angle shot as she crosses the frame. She is not looking at the camera. She is not looking at anything in particular. She is simply moving through space with total certainty.

Well-cut coat, single strand of pearls. Everything deliberate. The kind of woman who assembles people the way others arrange furniture — intuitively, correctly, once.

## Thoth — Database Specialist

Late 40s. Compact, meticulous. The type of face you'd describe as careful — every expression considered before it lands. Round wire-rimmed glasses. A thin moustache, immaculate. He carries the mild look of a man who has already found the error you haven't noticed yet.

Photographed at a desk — stacks of papers and filing systems visible behind him. He is holding a single document up to the light, examining it with an expression of intense, quiet satisfaction. As if the document has confirmed something he suspected all along.

Shirtsleeves rolled precisely to the same point on each forearm. A mechanical pencil tucked behind one ear. The desk lamp throws hard shadows. Everything around him is ordered — deliberately, almost aggressively so.

## Iris — Frontend Developer

Late 20s to early 30s. Sharp-featured, attentive. The kind of face that processes visual information differently from other people — she looks at things and sees their structure. There is something slightly restless about her energy, as if she is always a half-second ahead of the conversation.

Photographed in profile at what might be a drafting table or light board, bent slightly forward, examining something in close detail. Her expression is focused, neutral, absorbed. One hand traces something across the surface she's studying.

Dark turtleneck. Hair loose, tucked behind one ear. The light source is from below-left, giving the image a high-contrast, almost architectural quality. The background is dark and undefined.

## Argus — Web Harvester

Early 40s. Unremarkable by design. This is someone who has spent years perfecting the art of looking like no one in particular — medium height, medium build, forgettable coat, the kind of hat that could place him in any decade. And yet the eyes. The eyes are extraordinary: still, patient, missing nothing. He is not watching you. He is watching everything.

Photographed from a considerable distance — probably telephoto again. He is seated in a public space (a park bench, or possibly a bus stop) with a newspaper in his lap, but the newspaper is clearly incidental. He has been there for some time. He will be there for longer.

The image is grainier than the others — maximum telephoto grain. He appears to know he is being photographed and to have decided, philosophically, that this is fine.

## Mycroft — QA & Data Quality Specialist

Late 50s. Large. Not tall necessarily, but substantial — the presence of a man who occupies space with complete comfort and no apology. Heavy-lidded eyes that convey, with considerable precision, that he has already thought of the objection you are about to raise. A deep vertical crease between the brows. He is never in a hurry.

Photographed in an armchair — the one image in the set that feels almost staged, except that it isn't. He is holding a typed report at reading distance, one leg crossed, expression somewhere between amusement and contempt. Not for the person who wrote it. For the error on page three.

Dark suit, pocket square, reading glasses low on his nose. A telephone on the side table. The room behind him is mahogany and leather, implying significant institutional authority. The photograph has the quality of something taken through a two-way mirror.

## Cicero — Prompt Engineer

Mid-40s. Precise, contained energy. The face of an orator at rest: strong jaw, expressive mouth held deliberately still. The kind of man who, when he finally speaks, has already edited the sentence three times in his head. His stillness in the photograph reads not as passivity but as compression — something held.

Photographed at a small table, apparently in the act of dictating or composing. His mouth is slightly open — he is mid-word. His eyes are fixed on the middle distance, focused on something internal. One hand is raised, index finger slightly extended, the classic gesture of someone calibrating precisely what they mean to say.

Dark jacket, white shirt, no tie. A legal pad and several annotated papers on the table. The image is caught slightly motion-blurred — Cicero in the act of thinking is not quite still.
